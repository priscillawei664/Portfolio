I.

A Few Words

Branding is far more than a visual identity. It is built through consistent, intentional interactions that earn trust, shape desire, and hold loyalty over time.

P. Wei

Hi, I'm Guanqiao (Priscilla) Wei. I'm a Sydney-based Client Advisor at Chanel. The past year and a half on the floor have given me a close, everyday view of how a high-touch luxury environment really works: client engagement, brand representation, and the quieter craft of experiential storytelling.

Working closely with clients on the floor has shaped how I think about branding. Supporting in-store activations and campaign execution has taught me how to turn strategy into something people actually feel. Along the way, I pay close attention to how customers move, what makes them pause, and what pulls them back.

Alongside my role in luxury retail, I work as a freelance designer in branding and visual communication, mostly with charities and non-profit organisations. The work ranges from brand identity development to multi-channel communication assets, and the through-line is always the same: a cohesive, insight-led visual system that feels considered.

A recent highlight was contributing to the 50th anniversary milestone celebration for St Laurence House, where I supported visual identity and communication materials across digital and print. The goal was simple: the same story, told the same way, whether someone read an invitation, a commemorative book, or a social post.

My focus sits at the intersection of branding, creative strategy, luxury marketing, and communications. I care about how brands stay relevant and earn emotional connection through experiences that feel coherent from the first impression to the thirtieth.
